# Erdős Problem #114

## Title
Polynomial level-curve length

## Status
Open (falsifiable)

## Prize
$250

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/114

## Problem statement

If $p(z)\in\mathbb{C}[z]$ is a monic polynomial of degree $n$ then is the length of the curve
\[
\{ z\in \mathbb{C} : \lvert p(z)\rvert=1\}
\]
maximised when $p(z)=z^n-1$?

## What is being measured?

For a monic polynomial \(p(z)\) of degree \(n\), look at the level curve

\[
|p(z)|=1
\]

in the complex plane.

The problem asks which monic polynomial makes this curve as long as possible.

The conjectured extremal example is

\[
p(z)=z^n-1.
\]

## Known context

Let \(f(n)\) be the maximal possible length of the curve \(|p(z)|=1\), over all monic degree-\(n\) polynomials.

For \(p(z)=z^n-1\), the length is

\[
2n+O(1).
\]

Known progress includes:

\[
f(n)\leq 4\pi n,
\]

\[
f(n)\leq 2\pi n,
\]

and the asymptotic result

\[
f(n)\leq 2n+O(n^{7/8}).
\]

The source page also notes recent work proving that \(p(z)=z^n-1\) is the unique maximiser, up to rotation and translation, for all sufficiently large \(n\).

## Visual idea

Draw the complex plane with a lemniscate-like level curve \(|p(z)|=1\).

Then show the special polynomial

\[
z^n-1
\]

as producing a symmetric multi-lobed curve whose length is conjecturally maximal.

The poster should visually emphasize:
- monic degree-\(n\) polynomials,
- the level curve \(|p(z)|=1\),
- curve length,
- the extremal candidate \(z^n-1\),
- and the recent asymptotic / large-\(n\) progress.
