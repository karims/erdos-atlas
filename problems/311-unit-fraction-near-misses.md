# Erdős Problem #311

## Title
Unit fraction near-misses

## Status
Open

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/311

## Problem statement

Let $\delta(N)$ be the minimal non-zero value of
\[
\left|1-\sum_{n\in A}\frac{1}{n}\right|
\]
as $A$ ranges over all subsets of $\{1,\ldots,N\}$. Is it true that
\[
\delta(N)=e^{-(c+o(1))N}
\]
for some constant $c\in(0,1)$?

## Visual idea

Choose a subset \(A\subseteq\{1,\ldots,N\}\), then add the unit fractions

\[
\sum_{n\in A}\frac{1}{n}.
\]

The value \(\delta(N)\) measures the closest nonzero miss from exactly \(1\).

The poster should visually emphasize:
- unit fractions as small pieces,
- subset sums trying to land on \(1\),
- the closest nonzero gap \(\delta(N)\),
- and the exponential scale \(e^{-cN}\).
