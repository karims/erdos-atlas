# Erdős Problem #509

## Title
Polynomial lemniscate covering

## Status
Open

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/509

## Problem statement

Let $f(z)\in\mathbb{C}[z]$ be a monic non-constant polynomial. Can the set
\[
\{ z\in \mathbb{C} : \lvert f(z)\rvert \leq 1\}
\]
be covered by a set of circles the sum of whose radii is $\leq 2$?

## What is being covered?

Given a monic non-constant complex polynomial \(f(z)\), consider its unit sublevel set:

\[
L_f=\{z\in\mathbb C:|f(z)|\leq 1\}.
\]

This is a polynomial lemniscate.

The problem asks whether \(L_f\) can always be covered by circles \(D_i\) whose total radius satisfies

\[
\sum_i r_i\leq 2.
\]

## Known context

Cartan proved this kind of covering statement with \(2\) replaced by \(2e\).

Pommerenke improved the constant to \(2.59\).

Pommerenke also proved that the constant \(2\) is achievable when the sublevel set is connected.

## Visual idea

Show a complex plane with a lemniscate-shaped region, possibly with several components.

Then cover it using circles whose radii add up to at most \(2\).

The poster should visually emphasize:
- a monic polynomial \(f(z)\),
- the sublevel region \(|f(z)|\leq 1\),
- a circle covering of that region,
- the total radius budget \(\sum r_i\leq 2\),
- and the open question of whether the sharp constant \(2\) always works.
