# Erdős Problem #1142

## Title
Prime after powers of two

## Status
Open

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/1142

## Problem statement

Are there infinitely many $n$ (or any $n>105$) such that $n-2^k$ is prime for all $1<2^k<n$?

## Visual idea

Fix an integer $n$.

Look backward from $n$ by subtracting every power of two below it:

\[
n-2,\quad n-4,\quad n-8,\quad n-16,\quad \ldots
\]

The question asks whether all of these numbers can be prime.

The known examples stop at $105$, making the poster feel like a search for a rare integer whose “power-of-two shadows” all land on primes.
