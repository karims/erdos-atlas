# Erdős Problem #810

## Title
Rainbow four-cycles

## Status
Open

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/810

## Problem statement

Does there exist some $\epsilon>0$ such that, for all sufficiently large $n$, there exists a graph $G$ on $n$ vertices with at least $\epsilon n^2$ many edges such that the edges can be coloured with $n$ colours so that every $C_4$ receives $4$ distinct colours?

## Visual idea

We seek a dense graph on \(n\) vertices, with at least \(\epsilon n^2\) edges.

The edges are coloured using only \(n\) colours.

The condition is that every copy of \(C_4\), a four-cycle, must be rainbow: its four edges all have distinct colours.

The poster should visually emphasize:
- a dense graph,
- edge-colouring with \(n\) colours,
- a highlighted \(C_4\),
- and the constraint that each \(C_4\) has four distinct edge colours.
