# Erdős Problem #1168

## Title
A partition relation without GCH

## Status
Open

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/1168

## Problem statement

Prove that
\[
\aleph_{\omega+1}\not\to (\aleph_{\omega+1}, 3,\ldots,3)_{\aleph_0}^2
\]
without assuming the generalised continuum hypothesis.

## What is being asked?

This is a set-theoretic Ramsey problem about colourings of pairs.

The notation

\[
\kappa\to(\lambda,\mu_0,\mu_1,\ldots)^2_\theta
\]

is a partition relation. Roughly, it says that every colouring of pairs from \(\kappa\) into \(\theta\) colours must contain a large homogeneous structure of one of the listed types.

The problem asks for a proof of a negative relation at

\[
\kappa=\aleph_{\omega+1}
\]

using countably many colours, without relying on the generalised continuum hypothesis.

## Visual idea

Show a vast infinite cardinal tower:

\[
\aleph_0,\aleph_1,\aleph_2,\ldots,\aleph_\omega,\aleph_{\omega+1}.
\]

At the top, draw a colouring of pairs using countably many colours.

The poster should visually emphasize:
- the cardinal \(\aleph_{\omega+1}\),
- pair-colourings with \(\aleph_0\) colours,
- the negative partition arrow \(\not\to\),
- the avoidance of large homogeneous configurations,
- and the removal of the GCH assumption.
