# Erdős Problem #1186

## Title
Monochromatic progressions in 2-colourings

## Status
Open

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/1186

## Problem statement

Let $\delta_k$ be such that in any $2$-colouring of $\{1,\ldots,n\}$ there exist at least $(\delta_k+o(1))n^2$ many monochromatic $k$-term arithmetic progressions. Give reasonable bounds (or even an asymptotic formula) for $\delta_k$.

## Visual idea

A row of integers from $1$ to $n$ is coloured in two colours.

Some equally spaced subsequences form arithmetic progressions.  
Among these, the monochromatic ones are counted.

The central object is $\delta_k$, the limiting lower-density constant for unavoidable monochromatic $k$-term arithmetic progressions.
