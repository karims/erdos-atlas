# Erdős Problem #169

## Title
Progression-free reciprocal sums

## Status
Open

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/169

## Problem statement

Let $k\geq 3$ and $f(k)$ be the supremum of $\sum_{n\in A}\frac{1}{n}$ as $A$ ranges over all sets of positive integers which do not contain a $k$-term arithmetic progression. Estimate $f(k)$.

Is
\[
\lim_{k\to \infty}\frac{f(k)}{\log W(k)}=\infty
\]
where $W(k)$ is the van der Waerden number?

## Visual idea

We search through sets \(A\subseteq\mathbb{N}\) that avoid \(k\)-term arithmetic progressions.

Among all such progression-free sets, \(f(k)\) measures how large the reciprocal sum

\[
\sum_{n\in A}\frac{1}{n}
\]

can become.

The poster should visually contrast:
- a forbidden \(k\)-term arithmetic progression,
- a scattered progression-free set \(A\),
- the reciprocal weights \(1/n\),
- and the comparison with \(\log W(k)\).
