# Erdős Problem #968

## Title
Prime index ratio increases

## Status
Open

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/forum/thread/968

## Problem statement

Let $u_n=p_n/n$, where $p_n$ is the $n$th prime. Does the set of $n$ such that $u_n<u_{n+1}$ have positive density?

## Visual idea

The sequence

\[
u_n=\frac{p_n}{n}
\]

measures the average size of the first \(n\) primes relative to the index \(n\).

The inequality

\[
u_n<u_{n+1}
\]

means the ratio increases from one prime index to the next.

Equivalently,

\[
\frac{p_n}{n}<\frac{p_{n+1}}{n+1}.
\]

The poster should visually emphasize:
- the sequence \(u_n=p_n/n\),
- occasional upward steps \(u_n<u_{n+1}\),
- the connection to prime gaps,
- and the density question: do such upward steps occur with positive density?
