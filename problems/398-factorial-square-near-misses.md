# Erdős Problem #398

## Title
Factorial square near-misses

## Status
Open (falsifiable)

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/398

## Problem statement

Are the only solutions to
\[
n!=x^2-1
\]
when $n=4,5,7$?

## Equivalent form

The equation can be rewritten as

\[
x^2=n!+1.
\]

So the problem asks when \(n!+1\) is a perfect square.

The known solutions are:

\[
4!+1=25=5^2,
\]

\[
5!+1=121=11^2,
\]

\[
7!+1=5041=71^2.
\]

## Visual idea

A factorial grows rapidly, while perfect squares form a separate ladder.

The problem asks whether \(n!+1\) lands on a square only three times.

The poster should visually emphasize:
- factorial values \(n!\),
- the “+1” jump,
- landing exactly on a square \(x^2\),
- the three known solutions \(n=4,5,7\),
- and the question: are there any more?
