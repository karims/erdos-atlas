# Erdős Problem #366

## Title
Full neighbours

## Status
Open (verifiable)

## Source
https://www.erdosproblems.com/366

## Problem statement

Are there any $2$-full $n$ such that $n+1$ is $3$-full? That is, if $p\mid n$ then $p^2\mid n$ and if $p\mid n+1$ then $p^3\mid n+1$.

## Visual idea

Two consecutive integers stand side by side.

The left integer is $2$-full: every prime dividing it appears with exponent at least $2$.
The right integer is $3$-full: every prime dividing it appears with exponent at least $3$.

The poster should visually emphasize:
- consecutive integers,
- prime-power structure,
- and the asymmetry between $2$-full and $3$-full.
