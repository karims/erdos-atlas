Rare distances

## Problem statement

Let $A\subset \mathbb{R}^2$ be a set of $n$ points. Must there be two distances which occur at least once but between at most $n$ pairs of points? Must the number of such distances $\to \infty$ as $n\to \infty$?

## Visual idea

Given \(n\) points in the plane, every pair of points determines a distance.

Some distances may occur many times; others may occur only a few times.

This problem asks whether there must always be at least two “rare” distances: distances that occur, but occur between at most \(n\) pairs of points.

The poster should visually emphasize:
- a cloud of points in the plane,
- repeated distance classes as arcs or chords,
- one guaranteed rare distance,
- the mystery of whether a second rare distance must always appear,
- and the stronger question of whether the number of rare distances tends to infinity.
