# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #1142

Create a vertical mathematical poster in the Erdős Atlas visual style:
ivory parchment background, black serif typography, deep red accents, delicate divider lines, elegant framing, and a refined mathematical-art composition.

Poster title:
Prime Shadows of Powers

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #1142

Small label:
Primes after subtracting powers of two

Use the exact problem statement in a compact problem panel:

Are there infinitely many $n$ (or any $n>105$) such that $n-2^k$ is prime for all $1<2^k<n$?

Main visual structure:
1. A panel titled “The Problem”.
2. A panel titled “What is being tested?” explaining:
   - choose an integer $n$
   - subtract every power of two below it
   - require each $n-2^k$ to be prime
3. A central visual:
   - place a large $n$ on the right side or upper center
   - draw backward arrows or rays labeled $2,4,8,16,\ldots$
   - each ray lands on a marked prime value $n-2^k$
4. A formula panel:
   \[
   n-2^k \text{ is prime for every } 1<2^k<n
   \]
5. A known examples panel:
   \[
   4,\ 7,\ 15,\ 21,\ 45,\ 75,\ 105
   \]
6. A mystery panel emphasizing:
   \[
   \text{Any } n>105?
   \]
   and
   \[
   \text{Infinitely many?}
   \]

Design rules:
- Keep the poster close to the initial Erdős Atlas style.
- Avoid a dense classroom infographic layout.
- Make the central idea visual: an integer \(n\) casting power-of-two shadows backward onto primes.
- Use only ivory, black, and deep red.
- Keep mathematical text crisp and legible.
