# Source notes

## Problem ID
1142

## Source link
https://www.erdosproblems.com/1142

## Exact statement

Are there infinitely many $n$ (or any $n>105$) such that $n-2^k$ is prime for all $1<2^k<n$?

## Short label
Prime after powers of two

## Status
Open

## Tags
number theory, primes

## Known examples

The site lists the only known such values as:

\[
4,\ 7,\ 15,\ 21,\ 45,\ 75,\ 105.
\]

It also states that there are no other such \(n\leq 2^{44}\).

## Visual metaphor

An integer \(n\) casts shadows backward by powers of two.  
The rare condition is that every shadow \(n-2^k\), for \(1<2^k<n\), lands on a prime.
