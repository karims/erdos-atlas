# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #114

Create a vertical mathematical poster in the Erdős Atlas visual style:
ivory parchment background, black serif typography, deep red accents, delicate divider lines, elegant framing, and a refined mathematical-art composition.

Poster title:
The Longest Polynomial Curve

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #114

Small label:
Level curves of monic complex polynomials

Use the exact problem statement in a compact problem panel:

If $p(z)\in\mathbb{C}[z]$ is a monic polynomial of degree $n$ then is the length of the curve
\[
\{ z\in \mathbb{C} : \lvert p(z)\rvert=1\}
\]
maximised when $p(z)=z^n-1$?

Main visual structure:
1. A panel titled “The Problem”.
2. A panel titled “The Level Curve” explaining:
   - \(p(z)\) is monic of degree \(n\),
   - look at the curve \(|p(z)|=1\),
   - measure its geometric length.
3. A central visual:
   - draw a complex plane,
   - draw a smooth red lemniscate-style level curve,
   - show a special symmetric multi-lobed curve labeled \(p(z)=z^n-1\),
   - mark the idea of length by small tangent/arc-length ticks along the curve.
4. A formula panel:
   \[
   L(p)=\operatorname{length}\{z\in\mathbb C:|p(z)|=1\}.
   \]
5. An extremal candidate panel:
   \[
   p(z)=z^n-1.
   \]
6. A known scale panel:
   \[
   L(z^n-1)=2n+O(1).
   \]
7. A progress panel:
   \[
   f(n)\leq 2n+O(n^{7/8})
   \]
   and note: the extremal result is known for all sufficiently large \(n\).

Design rules:
- Keep the house style close to the initial Erdős Atlas posters.
- Use ivory, black, and deep red.
- Make the central visual analytic and geometric, not crowded.
- Keep the complex-plane level curve visually central.
- Make \(p(z)=z^n-1\) and \(|p(z)|=1\) prominent.
