# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #810

Create a vertical mathematical poster in the Erdős Atlas visual style:
ivory parchment background, black serif typography, deep red accents, delicate divider lines, elegant framing, and a refined mathematical-art composition.

Poster title:
Rainbow Four-Cycles

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #810

Small label:
Dense graphs with rainbow \(C_4\)'s

Use the exact problem statement in a compact problem panel:

Does there exist some $\epsilon>0$ such that, for all sufficiently large $n$, there exists a graph $G$ on $n$ vertices with at least $\epsilon n^2$ many edges such that the edges can be coloured with $n$ colours so that every $C_4$ receives $4$ distinct colours?

Main visual structure:
1. A panel titled “The Problem”.
2. A panel titled “What is being asked?” explaining:
   - choose a graph \(G\) on \(n\) vertices,
   - require at least \(\epsilon n^2\) edges,
   - colour all edges using \(n\) colours,
   - every \(C_4\) must receive four distinct colours.
3. A central visual:
   - draw a dense graph as a network of many black/sepia vertices and thin edges,
   - highlight one four-cycle \(C_4\) as a square or diamond,
   - colour the four edges of that \(C_4\) in four distinct accent tones or four distinct line styles within the deep red/black palette.
4. A formula panel:
   \[
   e(G)\geq \epsilon n^2
   \]
   and
   \[
   C_4 \Rightarrow 4\text{ distinct edge colours}.
   \]
5. A question panel:
   \[
   \text{Can } n\text{ colours force every }C_4\text{ to be rainbow?}
   \]
6. Optional anti-Ramsey note:
   \[
   \chi_S(n,\epsilon n^2,C_4)\leq n?
   \]

Design rules:
- Keep the house style close to the initial Erdős Atlas posters.
- Do not use bright rainbow colours everywhere.
- Since the project palette is ivory, black, and deep red, represent “four distinct colours” using four distinguishable edge treatments: black, deep red, dark sepia, dashed red, or subtle gold-brown if needed.
- Keep the central \(C_4\) visually unmistakable.
- Make the poster elegant, graph-theoretic, and not overcrowded.
