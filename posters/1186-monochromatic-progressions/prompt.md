# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #1186

Create a vertical mathematical art poster in the same visual style as the Erdős Atlas series: ivory parchment background, black serif mathematical typography, deep red accents, delicate divider lines, and refined diagrammatic composition.

Poster title:
Monochromatic Progressions

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #1186

Small label:
Arithmetic progressions in 2-colourings

Use the exact problem statement, but keep it in a smaller readable problem box rather than making it dominate the poster:

Let $\delta_k$ be such that in any $2$-colouring of $\{1,\ldots,n\}$ there exist at least $(\delta_k+o(1))n^2$ many monochromatic $k$-term arithmetic progressions. Give reasonable bounds (or even an asymptotic formula) for $\delta_k$.

Main visual structure:

1. A horizontal number line labelled $1,\ldots,n$.
2. Points on the line are coloured only black and deep red, representing a 2-colouring.
3. Several equally spaced subsequences are highlighted as arithmetic progressions.
4. Red arcs connect one monochromatic red progression.
5. Black arcs connect one monochromatic black progression.
6. A central formula panel emphasizes:

\[
\#\{\text{monochromatic } k\text{-APs}\} \geq (\delta_k+o(1))n^2
\]

7. Make $\delta_k$ visually central, perhaps inside a small ornamental medallion or highlighted box.
8. Include a small explanatory formula:

\[
a,\ a+d,\ a+2d,\ \ldots,\ a+(k-1)d
\]

Design rules:

- Use only ivory, black, and deep red.
