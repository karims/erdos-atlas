# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #366

Create a vertical mathematical poster in the Erdős Atlas visual style:
ivory parchment background, black serif typography, deep red accents, delicate divider lines, elegant framing, and a refined mathematical-art composition.

Poster title:
Full Neighbours

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #366

Small label:
2-full and 3-full consecutive integers

Use the exact problem statement in a compact problem panel:

Are there any $2$-full $n$ such that $n+1$ is $3$-full? That is, if $p\mid n$ then $p^2\mid n$ and if $p\mid n+1$ then $p^3\mid n+1$.

Main visual structure:
1. A panel titled “The Problem”.
2. A panel explaining what “2-full” and “3-full” mean:
   - 2-full: every prime divisor appears to exponent at least 2
   - 3-full: every prime divisor appears to exponent at least 3
3. A central visual of two adjacent integer blocks:
   - left block labeled n
   - right block labeled n+1
4. Under the blocks, factorization-style examples using prime powers:
   - Example pair: 8 and 9
   - Optional second example pair: 12167 and 12168
5. A small visual cue showing:
   - n built from square-level prime powers
   - n+1 built from cube-level prime powers
6. A question panel emphasizing the rarity of such consecutive pairs.
