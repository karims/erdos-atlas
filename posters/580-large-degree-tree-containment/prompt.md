# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #580

Create a vertical mathematical poster in the Erdős Atlas visual style:
ivory parchment background, black serif typography, deep red accents, delicate divider lines, elegant framing, and a refined mathematical-art composition.

Poster title:
Do Large Degrees Force Every Tree?

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #580

Small label:
Embedding trees from many high-degree vertices

Use the exact problem statement in a compact problem panel:

Let $G$ be a graph on $n$ vertices such that at least $n/2$ vertices have degree at least $n/2$. Must $G$ contain every tree on at most $n/2$ vertices?

Main visual structure:
1. A panel titled “The Problem”.
2. A panel titled “Degree condition” explaining:
   - \(G\) has \(n\) vertices,
   - at least \(n/2\) vertices satisfy \(\deg(v)\geq n/2\).
3. A central visual:
   - draw a large graph \(G\),
   - highlight half of its vertices in deep red as high-degree vertices,
   - draw many thin incident edges from these high-degree vertices,
   - overlay or inset a tree \(T\) being embedded into \(G\).
4. A tree panel:
   \[
   |V(T)|\leq n/2
   \]
   with a small clean tree diagram.
5. A containment question panel:
   \[
   T\subseteq G\quad\text{for every tree }T\text{ on at most }n/2\text{ vertices?}
   \]
6. A context panel:
   - Erdős–Füredi–Loebl–Sós conjecture.
   - Holds for all sufficiently large \(n\) by Zhao.
7. Optional generalisation panel:
   \[
   \text{at least }n/2\text{ vertices have degree }\geq k
   \Rightarrow
   \text{contains every }k\text{-vertex tree?}
   \]

Footer:
Status badge: Decidable
Source: erdosproblems.com/580

Design rules:
- Keep the house style close to the initial Erdős Atlas posters.
- Use ivory, black, and deep red.
- Do not overcrowd the poster.
- Make the central visual graph-theoretic: a large graph, highlighted high-degree vertices, and a tree embedded inside.
- Keep the two thresholds \(n/2\) and \(n/2\) visually prominent.
