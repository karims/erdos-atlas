# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #222

Create a vertical mathematical poster in the Erdős Atlas visual style:
ivory parchment background, black serif typography, deep red accents, delicate divider lines, elegant framing, and a refined mathematical-art composition.

Poster title:
Gaps Between Two-Square Numbers

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #222

Small label:
Consecutive integers representable as \(a^2+b^2\)

Use the exact problem statement in a compact problem panel:

Let $n_1<n_2<\cdots$ be the sequence of integers which are the sum of two squares. Explore the behaviour of (i.e. find good upper and lower bounds for) the consecutive differences $n_{k+1}-n_k$.

Main visual structure:
1. A panel titled “The Problem”.
2. A panel titled “Which numbers count?” explaining:
   - mark integers representable as \(a^2+b^2\),
   - order them as \(n_1<n_2<\cdots\).
3. A central visual:
   - draw a horizontal number line,
   - mark sums of two squares as red/black points,
   - show a long highlighted empty interval between \(n_k\) and \(n_{k+1}\),
   - label the gap \(n_{k+1}-n_k\).
4. A representation panel:
   \[
   n=a^2+b^2.
   \]
5. A lower-bound panel:
   \[
   n_{k+1}-n_k
   \gg
   \frac{\log n_k}{\sqrt{\log\log n_k}}
   \quad\text{infinitely often}.
   \]
6. An upper-bound panel:
   \[
   n_{k+1}-n_k\ll n_k^{1/4}.
   \]
7. A mystery panel:
   \[
   \text{How large can the gaps really be?}
   \]

Design rules:
- Keep the house style close to the initial Erdős Atlas posters.
- Use ivory, black, and deep red.
- Do not overcrowd the poster.
- Make the central visual about a number line with rare long gaps.
- Keep \(a^2+b^2\) and \(n_{k+1}-n_k\) visually prominent.
