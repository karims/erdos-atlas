# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #234

Create a vertical mathematical infographic poster in the Erdős Atlas visual style:
ivory parchment background, black serif mathematical typography, deep red accents, elegant divider lines, and refined diagrammatic composition.

Poster title:
The Distribution of Normalized Prime Gaps

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #234

Small label:
Normalized prime gaps

Use the exact problem statement in a compact problem box:

For every $c\geq 0$ the density $f(c)$ of integers for which
\[
\frac{p_{n+1}-p_n}{\log n}< c
\]
exists and is a continuous function of $c$.

Main visual structure:
1. A box titled “The Problem”.
2. A box titled “What is being measured?” explaining the gap between consecutive primes and the normalized quantity
   \[
   g_n=\frac{p_{n+1}-p_n}{\log n}.
   \]
3. A small row/table of the first few primes and gaps.
4. A simple scatter-style panel or schematic showing normalized gaps versus index.
5. A histogram-like or density panel indicating the distribution of $g_n$.
6. A cumulative distribution panel emphasizing
   \[
   f(c)=\lim_{N\to\infty}\frac{1}{N}\#\{1\leq n\leq N:g_n<c\}.
   \]
7. A section explaining what the function $f(c)$ means and why continuity matters.
8. Bottom summary strip: the problem asks whether normalized prime gaps have a limiting continuous distribution.

Design rules:
- Keep the exact problem statement, but do not let it dominate the poster.
- The main visual focus should be the normalized gap formula and the function $f(c)$.
- Keep the Erdős Atlas parchment / black / deep-red series identity.
- Make it more explanatory than text-heavy.
