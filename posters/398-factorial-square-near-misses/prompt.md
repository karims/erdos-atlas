# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #398

Create a vertical mathematical poster in the Erdős Atlas visual style:
ivory parchment background, black serif typography, deep red accents, delicate divider lines, elegant framing, and a refined mathematical-art composition.

Poster title:
One Step from a Square

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #398

Small label:
When is \(n!+1\) a perfect square?

Use the exact problem statement in a compact problem panel:

Are the only solutions to
\[
n!=x^2-1
\]
when $n=4,5,7$?

Main visual structure:
1. A panel titled “The Problem”.
2. A panel titled “Equivalent form” explaining:
   - \(n!=x^2-1\),
   - equivalently \(n!+1=x^2\),
   - so the factorial is one less than a square.
3. A central visual:
   - draw a factorial staircase labeled \(4!,5!,7!\),
   - show a small “+1” step from each factorial,
   - each lands exactly on a square tile labeled \(5^2,11^2,71^2\).
4. A known solutions panel:
   \[
   4!+1=5^2,\qquad
   5!+1=11^2,\qquad
   7!+1=71^2.
   \]
5. A mystery panel:
   \[
   n!+1\stackrel{?}{=}x^2
   \]
   only for \(n=4,5,7\)?
6. Optional context panel:
   This is the Brocard-Ramanujan conjecture.
7. Optional search note:
   No other solutions are known below \(10^9\).

Footer:
Status badge: Open / Falsifiable
Source: erdosproblems.com/398

Design rules:
- Keep the house style close to the initial Erdős Atlas posters.
- Use ivory, black, and deep red.
- Do not overcrowd the poster.
- Make the main visual simple and strong: factorial \(+1\) landing on a square.
- Keep \(n!+1=x^2\) visually central.
