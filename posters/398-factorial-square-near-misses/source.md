# Source notes

## Problem ID
398

## Source link
https://www.erdosproblems.com/398

## Exact statement

Are the only solutions to
\[
n!=x^2-1
\]
when $n=4,5,7$?

## Short label
Factorial square near-misses

## Status
Open (falsifiable)

## Tags
number theory, factorials

## Notes

This is the Brocard-Ramanujan conjecture.

The known solutions are:

\[
4!=5^2-1,
\]

\[
5!=11^2-1,
\]

\[
7!=71^2-1.
\]

Equivalently:

\[
4!+1=5^2,\qquad
5!+1=11^2,\qquad
7!+1=71^2.
\]

The page notes that there are no other solutions below \(10^9\).

It also notes that Overholt showed there are only finitely many solutions assuming a weak form of the ABC conjecture.

## Visual metaphor

A factorial staircase climbs beside a square-number ladder.  
Only at rare points does \(n!+1\) land exactly on a square.
