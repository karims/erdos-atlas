# Source notes

## Problem ID
1168

## Source link
https://www.erdosproblems.com/1168

## Exact statement

Prove that
\[
\aleph_{\omega+1}\not\to (\aleph_{\omega+1}, 3,\ldots,3)_{\aleph_0}^2
\]
without assuming the generalised continuum hypothesis.

## Short label
Partition relation without GCH

## Status
Open

## Tags
set theory, Ramsey theory

## Notes

The page says this is a problem of Erdős, Hajnal, and Rado.

The problem concerns a negative partition relation at the successor of a singular cardinal:

\[
\aleph_{\omega+1}.
\]

The phrase “without assuming the generalised continuum hypothesis” is central: the challenge is to prove the negative relation in ZFC, rather than under GCH.

## Visual metaphor

An infinite Ramsey arrow breaks at \(\aleph_{\omega+1}\).  
The question is whether this break can be proved without the scaffolding of GCH.
