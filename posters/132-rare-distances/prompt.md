# Poster prompt: Erdős Problem #132

Create a vertical mathematical poster in the Erdős Atlas visual style:
ivory parchment background, black serif typography, deep red accents, delicate divider lines, elegant framing, and a refined mathematical-art composition.

Poster title:
Rare Distances

Subtitle:
Erdős Problem #132

Small label:
Distances determined by finite point sets

Use the exact problem statement in a compact problem panel:

Let $A\subset \mathbb{R}^2$ be a set of $n$ points. Must there be two distances which occur at least once but between at most $n$ pairs of points? Must the number of such distances $\to \infty$ as $n\to \infty$?

Main visual structure:
1. A panel titled “The Problem”.
2. A panel titled “What is being counted?” explaining:
   - choose \(n\) points in the plane,
   - every pair of points determines a distance,
   - count how many pairs realize each distance.
3. A central visual:
   - draw a finite constellation of black points in the plane,
   - connect some pairs with thin chords/arcs,
   - use repeated line styles to show one distance occurring many times,
   - highlight two rare distances in deep red.
4. A definition panel:
   \[
   1\leq m(d)\leq n
   \]
   where \(m(d)\) is the number of pairs at distance \(d\).
5. A question panel:
   \[
   \text{Must there be at least two such distances?}
   \]
6. A growth question panel:
   \[
   \#\{d:1\leq m(d)\leq n\}\to\infty?
   \]
7. Optional note panel:
   The largest distance is known to occur at most \(n\) times; the mystery is whether another rare distance must exist.
